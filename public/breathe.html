Tree of Life uses inclusive, multi-sensory balloon artistry to transform public spaces into vibrant hubs of connection and wonder. By combining creative workshops with a charitable heart, this award-winning initiative fosters community cohesion and provides meaningful emotional support to diverse groups across the region.

Twist, Connect, and Make a Difference! Unlock a new skill, find your community, and turn creativity into care.

Are you looking for a fun way to de-stress, meet kind-hearted people, and make a real impact?

Day 1: Balloon Twisting Training (June 19th)

Unwind and Create: Join us for a relaxing afternoon where creativity meets community. No artistic background? No problem! Whether you are a total beginner or just looking to take a break from the books, our friendly instructors will guide you through simple, fun techniques to twist balloons into animals, flowers, and more.

Day 2: Charity Fundraising Sale (June 20th)

Put Skills to Action: Now that you have mastered the twist, it is time to use your talent for good! Join our volunteer team for a special charity fundraising event.

Change Lives: Every balloon we twist is an opportunity to support the Sickle Cell Society. Your effort will directly help raise vital funds and awareness for those affected by Sickle Cell. Together, we can turn simple balloons into a powerful force for change.

Moments of Calm Programme

Location Guildford, Surrey
Type Creative Workshop
Cost Free
Time Ongoing Series

Crafting Smiles is a non-profit creative ecosystem that connects people through handmade crafts to foster wellbeing, community belonging, and social contribution. By creating simple items like Worry Worms and Calm Balls, volunteers provide essential emotional support to those in healthcare, education, and community settings across the UK.

Bringing comfort in stressful moments. Through simple handmade items—such as Worry Worms and Calm Balls—we help students create small moments of reassurance.

Our creations currently support:

  • Healthcare: Patients and families in hospitals and wellbeing programmes.
  • Youth Support: Young people in mental resilience initiatives (e.g., BRIYM).
  • Education: Emotional support settings in schools.
  • Community: Individuals and families in need of comfort.

Learn CPR in 15 Minutes. Save a Life.

Organization British Heart Foundation
Format Online Training
Cost Free
Time Ongoing Series

Free, trusted online CPR training by the British Heart Foundation. Every second counts.

Cardiac arrest can happen to anyone, anywhere, at any time. In these critical moments, knowing CPR can mean the difference between life and death. The British Heart Foundation's quick 15-minute training module equips you with the essential knowledge and confidence to act when it matters most.

What You'll Learn:

• How to recognize the signs of cardiac arrest
• Step-by-step CPR techniques
• How to use an Automated External Defibrillator (AED)
• What to do in an emergency
• When and how to call emergency services

Why This Matters: While medical professionals are on their way, CPR from a trained bystander can keep blood flowing to vital organs, significantly improving the chances of survival. You don't need to be a medical professional—you just need to be willing to help.

In partnership with Breathe and the broader youth wellness community, we believe that equipping young people with life-saving skills strengthens our collective ability to support one another in times of crisis.
