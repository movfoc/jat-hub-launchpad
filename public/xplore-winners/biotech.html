Innovation Meets Impact: Student-Led Project "Jathub X-plore" Crowned Winner of Ideathon

LONDON & HONG KONG

Jathub CIC and University College London (UCL) are proud to announce the victory of the Kowloon True Light School team in the recent Jathub X-plore Ideathon. Their groundbreaking project, which synthesizes advanced AI-driven biotechnology with innovative public health communication, has set a new benchmark for youth-led scientific inquiry.

The Ideathon was established to challenge the next generation of researchers to solve complex global health problems through interdisciplinary collaboration. The winning team distinguished itself by not only delivering a rigorous therapeutic solution for lung cancer but also by successfully translating complex laboratory data into accessible, gamified educational tools.

Technical Breakthrough: A New Frontier in Oncology

The team addressed the challenge of KRAS-mutated lung cancer, a condition often characterized by resistance to traditional therapies. Their research focused on targeting PD-delta, a protein responsible for transporting the KRAS oncogene to the cell membrane where it initiates tumor growth.

By utilizing advanced computational tools—including protein MPNN and DynamicBind—the students engineered a novel peptide inhibitor, FPB1-CPP. This therapeutic intervention demonstrated significant potential in laboratory trials:

  • Potency: In A549 cell line testing, the peptide induced an 80% reduction in cancer cell viability.
  • Synergy: When utilized in combination with the chemotherapy agent cisplatin, the treatment showed a synergistic effect, offering the possibility of lower required dosages and reduced systemic side effects.
  • Safety: The drug exhibited a high selectivity profile, demonstrating no significant toxicity to non-cancerous bronchial epithelial cells.

Bridging the Science-Society Gap

Beyond the laboratory, the project's success is rooted in its "Four Pillars of Research": Generation, Expression, Networking, and Education. The judges were particularly impressed by the team's commitment to "Science for Everyone."

To bridge the gap between academic research and the public, the team launched a multi-platform educational suite:

  • Gamification: The team developed Cancer Cell Siege, an immersive shooter game on the Roblox platform. By gamifying cancer biology, they have successfully turned complex cellular mutation data into an interactive experience for millions of potential users.
  • Interactive Learning: Through the creation of Poke Acts, a proprietary card game, the students have successfully mapped real lab data onto competitive gameplay, allowing peers to engage with biotechnology through active learning rather than passive observation.
  • AI-Enhanced Communication: The team showcased an expert use of emerging technologies, including AlphaFold for 3D protein visualization and various AI-marketing tools to transform scientific statistics into compelling, professional visual narratives.

Significance of the Achievement

The success of the Kowloon True Light School team represents the core mission of the Jathub X-plore Ideathon: empowering students to take agency in scientific innovation.

"This project is a testament to the power of interdisciplinary thinking," stated a spokesperson for the organizing committee. "The team has demonstrated that cutting-edge biotech does not need to be sequestered within the walls of a lab. By meeting the public where they are—through gaming, digital hubs, and intuitive design—these students are not just researchers; they are the future of science communication."

As the winners of this year's Ideathon, the team serves as a beacon for high school innovators globally, proving that with the right application of AI and creative pedagogy, complex medical challenges can be demystified and addressed with ingenuity and passion.
